INTRODUCTION

What is ?

Animation is the art of making static images appear to move by displaying them in a rapid sequence. It bridges the gap between imagination and reality, bringing characters and worlds to life through motion.

Types of Animation

2D Animation

Hand-drawn or vector-based movement on a flat plane. Great for storytelling and stylized characters.

Classic Examples: The Lion King, Rick & Morty

3D Animation

Computer-generated imagery with depth and complex 3D modeling. Used in blockbuster films and gaming.

Modern Examples: Toy Story, Frozen

Stop Motion

Physical objects moved in small increments between frames to create the illusion of movement.

Unique Examples: Coraline, Wallace & Gromit

VFX & Motion

Digital effects and animated graphic design. Essential for interfaces, titles, and live-action integration.

Commercial Examples: Marvel VFX, Movie Titles
