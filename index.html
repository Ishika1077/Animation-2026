MASTER CLASS

The 12 Principles of Animation

Master the fundamental techniques that bring characters to life. Defined by Disney animators, these principles are the bible of animation.

Start Learning Watch Video Intro
01

Squash and Stretch

This principle shows how objects change shape when they move, like a bouncing ball squashing on the ground and stretching in the air. It helps make animations feel more flexible, alive, and realistic.

02

Anticipation

Anticipation prepares the audience for an action before it happens, such as a character bending knees before jumping. It makes movements clearer and more believable.

03

Staging

Staging is about presenting an idea clearly by using proper camera angles, lighting, and character placement. It helps the audience understand what is important in a scene.

04

Straight Ahead & Pose to Pose

Straight ahead action means animating frame by frame from start to end, while pose to pose focuses on drawing key poses first. Both methods help control motion and timing in different ways.

05

Follow Through

When a character stops moving, parts like hair or clothes continue moving slightly. This principle adds realism by showing that not all parts stop at the same time.

06

Slow In & Slow Out

Movements usually start slowly, speed up, and then slow down again. Adding more frames at the beginning and end makes actions look smooth and natural.

07

Arcs

Most natural movements follow curved paths rather than straight lines. Using arcs makes character motion look more fluid and lifelike.

08

Secondary Actions

Secondary actions support the main action, like facial expressions while walking. They add detail and make characters more interesting without distracting from the main movement.

09

Timing

Timing refers to how many frames are used for an action, which affects speed and weight. Good timing makes movements feel realistic and helps show emotions and personality.

10

Exaggeration

Exaggeration means pushing movements or expressions slightly beyond reality. It makes animations more entertaining and helps emotions and actions stand out clearly.

11

Solid Drawing

Solid drawing focuses on creating characters with proper structure, balance, and weight. It helps animators draw characters that feel three-dimensional and stable.

12

Appeal
