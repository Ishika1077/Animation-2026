Stop Motion Animation

The art of physical movement. Discover the magic behind bringing inanimate objects to life, one frame at a time.

What is Stop Motion?

The Art of Physical Movement

Stop motion is an animated filmmaking technique in which objects are physically manipulated in small increments between frames. When played back rapidly, the objects appear to move independently.

Object Animation Claymation Pixilation

Key Characteristics

Frame Rate (FPS)

Typically shot at 12 or 24 frames per second. Shooting on “twos” creates a distinctive handcrafted charm.

Tactile Materials

Uses real-world objects like clay, puppets, paper cutouts, or LEGO. Imperfections add character.

Controlled Lighting

Requires consistent lighting conditions. Even slight flickers can affect the final animation.

How It Works

1

Concept & Storyboard

Every shot is meticulously planned. Timing is calculated down to the exact frame.

2

Fabrication

Puppets and sets are built. Armatures are used to hold poses for long periods.

3

Shooting

The animator moves the object slightly, captures a frame, and repeats the process thousands of times.
