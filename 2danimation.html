What is 2D Animation?

2D animation is a form of animation in which characters, objects, and environments are created and animated in a two-dimensional space, meaning they have only height and width and do not possess real depth like three-dimensional objects. In 2D animation, movement is created by displaying a sequence of images or drawings in rapid succession, with each frame showing slight changes from the previous one to give the illusion of motion. This type of animation can be produced using traditional hand-drawn techniques, where each frame is drawn individually, or through modern digital methods that use computer software to draw, rig, and animate characters. 2D animation is widely used in cartoons, television shows, films, educational content, advertisements, video games, and explainer videos because of its expressive artistic style, flexibility, and relatively lower production cost compared to 3D animation. It allows animators to emphasize creativity, emotion, and storytelling through stylized visuals, making it one of the most popular and enduring forms of animation.
